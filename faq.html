How Our Pet Waste Removal Service Works

How does your pet waste removal service work?

I come to your yard on a scheduled day, scoop all visible dog waste, secure it in bags, and place in your bin. For many clients in San Luis Obispo County, weekly service keeps the yard clean, sanitary, and guest-ready. One-time and first-time deep cleanups are also available.

Do I need to be home during service?

No. You do not need to be home as long as I can safely access the yard. Most clients are at work or out running errands while I handle the cleanup.

How do you handle locked gates?

If you have a gate code, lockbox, or key, you can share that securely with me when you start service. I never climb fences or force gates. If access is blocked, I’ll contact you to reschedule.

What do you do with the dog waste?

By default,I double bag all pet waste then place it in your designated outdoor trash bin.

Sanitation, Safety, and Eco-Friendly Practices

Is your deodorizer safe for pets, kids, and plants?

Yes. I use a gentle, outdoor-friendly deodorizing treatment designed to help neutralize tough pet odors at their source rather than just masking them. It’s suitable for use around pets, kids, lawns, and gardens, and does not harm your plants or landscaping when applied as directed.

We use a professional-grade deodorizing solution made from salt and water, activated to create hypochlorous acid (HOCl) — a naturally occurring compound that eliminates odor-causing bacteria and breaks down back into salt and water.

Do you sanitize your tools between yards?

Yes. Scoopers and tools are sanitized between properties to help minimize tracking bacteria or odors from one yard to another. Keeping equipment clean is part of keeping your yard clean.

Will you deodorize patios, concrete, or high-traffic areas?

Yes. Upon request, I can apply an outdoor deodorizing treatment to patios, concrete, and common dog areas to help reduce lingering odors. This is especially helpful for high-traffic potty spots that tend to hold onto smells.
